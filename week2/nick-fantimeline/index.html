Satoru Iwata

Satoru Iwata was a Japanese video game programmer who served as the fourth CEO of Nintendo.

Accomplishments

2000

Iwata takes seat as head of Nintendo's corporate division and takes a seat on the board of directors.

2002

Nintendo president Yamauchi retires and Iwata takes his place as the next CEO of Nintendo.

2004

Released Nintendo DS, leading the revitalization of Nintendo's handheld business.

2005

Introduced new program in Nintendo in which employees not asscoiated with game development could submit ideas for games.

2005

Released the Nintendo Wii, which eventually popularized motion control in video games, and almost doubled Nintendo's stock price.

2014

Iwata released a ten-year strategy for Nintendo based on quality of life products targeting a market outside of video games.
